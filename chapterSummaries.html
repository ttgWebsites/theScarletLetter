Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter #1
  • It is the 1600’s and a crowd has gathered outside of a prison in Boston
  • The large prison, designed to house dangerous criminals, sets an ominous scene
  • Next to the large oak door to the prison is a rose bush, as the narrator puts it, it is a reminder of nature’s kindness to the condemned
  • Chapter #2
  • The crowd is awaiting the arrival of Hester Prynne to the scaffold where she is to be condemned and her fate for committing adultery will be announced
  • Hester comes outside with a scarlet and gold colored “A” stitched to her dress, standing for adulterer, the women of the crowd jeer at her for this
  • The reader can determine through this scene that Hester has had an illegitimate child
  • Hester is finally called forth on the scaffold, children in the crowd taunt her as all the adults stare
  • At this time her life flashes before her, her growing up, her meeting a older scholar, and marrying him, traveling from Europe to Boston
  • Finally she is brought back to reality, and accidentally squeezes her daughter a little to tightly causing her to cry
  • Hester can not believe her current fate
  • Chapter #3
  • While looking out at the crowd Hester sees her husband, who she believed had sent her to America and was not following behind
  • He seems to be disguised but Hester sees right through it
  • He gestures at Hester to not reveal who he really is, he currently calls himself Roger Chillingworth
  • While in the crowd he questions others about Hester’s crimes learning that she has slept with another man, who is unknown, while her scholarly husband remains in Europe
  • Chillingworth states that her husband must have been foolish to believe an old man could keep her satisfied, he then asks who the father of her child is
  • The man tells Chillingworth that she will not reveal her lover, and that for punishment she must stand on the Scaffold for three hours and wear the scarlet letter upon her chest forever
  • Governor Bellingham, Reverend Wilson, and Reverend Dimmesdale, the towns fathers, are now introduced who sit and judge Hester
  • Dimmesdale, a young man who has been known for his wonderfully eloquent services, asks Hester to reveal her lover, but she refuses, and he pushes no further
  • Wilson then leads a sermon condemning sinners, constantly referring to Hester, who stand by idly and quiet
  • The sermon ends and Hester is led back to prison
  • Chapter #4
  • While in prison Hester and her husband confront one another, as he is brought inside for medical assistance, as he is passing himself off as a doctor
  • Chillingworth offers Hester medicine, which she fears is poison, yet he tells her that he has no need to poison her because he wants to live so he can have his revenge
  • He demands she tell him who her lover is but she refuses, Chillingworth decides he will seek out her lover himself and once he learns who he is exact his revenge
  • He also warns Hester against revealing his real identity saying it will only make life more difficult
  • Hester is very upset and frustrated with the way her husband is acting, going so far to suggest he is the devil in disguise
  • Chapter #5
  • After a few months in prison Hester is finally released, she decides to remain in Boston at the edge of the community in an old abandon cabin
  • She lives an alienated life as the community does not accept her or respect her, and there is a constant mark on her dress of her transgressions
  • She is used as an example of how not to live ones life
  • Hester provides for herself and daughter by her skilled needle work, even though she is an outcast her skills still bring in customers, even the Governor pays her for work
  • She works for all types of situations stitching clothes, except for wedding which she has been forbidden
  • Hester is still alienated even though she has plenty of work, those she works for still insult her and no one respects her
  • Chapter #6
  • More is learned about Hester’s daughter, Pearl
  • She is thus named Pearl because Hester gave up all she had to have the child, and she broke a major law in doing so
  • Pearl is quite different from normal children, not to mention she is an outcast because of her background as well
  • She is very mischievous, and also has developed much of Hester’s traits such including her moodiness, passion, and defiance
  • When taught about God, Pearl denies the teachings saying she doesn’t have a heavenly father
  • Pearl do to the lack of socialization she is allowed because of her mother, creates her own friends, a group of characters made up in her imagination
  • Pearl has a fascination with her mother’s scarlet letter often playing with it, one day to the point of Hester becoming upset and questioning what kind of person Pearl is
  • Pearl responds with the same question, wondering her origins, at this time Hester wonders if what the townspeople say is true, that Pearl is a demon child
  • Chapter #7
  • Hester heads to the Governor’s manor to deliver a pair of gloves and also to ask him if the rumors that Pearl will be taken away are true
  • It is belief that if Pearl is considered a demon she will be taken away for Hester’s sake, but if not she will be placed in a better home for her sake
  • While on the way Hester and Pearl are attacked by a group of children who fling mud at them
  • Pearl frightens the children away
  • When they arrive at the mansion it is described as a very large and well decorated home, built like an English aristocrat’s home, and decorated with large portraits, and a suit of armor
  • Hester looks at her reflection in the armor and is demoralized by the fact that her scarlet letter dominates the reflection
  • Pearl, excited, begins to demand a rose from a bush outside, but is silent as a group of men enter
  • Chapter #8
  • The group of men, Chillingworth, Dimmesdale, Wilson, and Bellingham, tease Pearl calling her a demon child, and comparing her to a bird
  • They then turn to Hester and ask why she is allowed to keep a child, and Hester responds by saying she can teach a lesson of her sin so as to keep the child from ever committing such sins
  • The men then test Pearl asking her religious questions, which she can not even answer the easiest of them
  • As a last ditch effort to keep her daughter Hester turns to Dimmesdale, he manages to convince the other man that Pearl should remain with Hester
  • Hester is delighted, but Chillingworth is furious, demanding that they reopen the investigation into who the father is, however he is denied
  • As Hester is leaving Mistress Hibbins confronts her asking her to attend a witches’ gathering, which Hester refuses on account of her daughter
  • Chapter #9
  • This chapter tells more about Chillingworth’s past and how he passes himself off as a doctor
  • Chillingworth had studied some remedial medicine back in Europe as well as worked with natives and learned about healing
  • He uses this to become the town doctor as no previous one was in place
  • Dimmesdale seems to become ill, and has had chest pains, so Chillingworth begins to keep a close eye on him
  • The two men move into the same house, that of a widow, where Chillingworth can watch over him
  • Though at first everyone is thrilled by this, they soon begin to see a darker side of Chillingworth, and he seems to develop the face of an evil person, prompting townspeople to refer to him as the Devil, and fear he may be harming Dimmesdale
  • Chapter #10
  • Chillingworth begins to devote all his time to finding the cause and cure of Dimmesdale’s illness
  • He even goes so far to try and discover all of Dimmesdale personal details, however Dimmesdale is not willing to share them
  • One day the two men get into a discussion of sin and confession, during this they witness Pearl playing in the nearby graveyard and sticking burrs on Hester’s scarlet letter
  • Pearl sees the men and turns to her mother dragging her away, Pearl is afraid the “black man” will get to them like he has gotten to the Reverend, the “black man” being Chillingworth
  • Dimmesdale uses Hester as an example saying she is not a women of un-confessed sin, for she wears her sin on her dress each day
  • Chillingworth is becoming more and more suspicious of Dimmesdale and his belief that perhaps he is the mysterious lover
  • He presses Dimmesdale for answers but receives none, finally he reaches the point where he waits till Dimmesdale is asleep and sneaks up to him, he looks under his shirt and sees something that causes Chillingworth to rejoice, however what it is still unknown to the reader
  • Chapter #11
  • Chillingworth torments Dimmesdale with mind games, causing Dimmesdale to suffer, as well as lose trust for him, and even loathe him
  • However Dimmesdale can find no rational truth to his feelings of loathing towards Chillingworth, and therefore must dismiss it
  • This though does not make the suffering any less easier for Dimmesdale
  • The only benefit is that Dimmesdale has had his most powerful sermons as of late
  • Dimmesdale argues with himself on whether he should confess but can not, this creates a major conflict within himself
  • Dimmesdale begins to suffer visions of Hester and Pearl, and he is suffering greatly, so much so that he decides to punish himself physically in hopes it will alleviate his suffering
  • He whips, starves, and keeps himself up all night meditating, all in an effort to alleviate pain, yet this does not work
  • He ultimately decides to hold his own visual on the scaffold where Hester was punished years before
  • Chapter #12
  • Dimmesdale puts himself on the scaffold and at first cries out because it causes him great pain, yet this goes unnoticed
  • He also is nearly noticed by the other Reverend yet luckily is not
  • Eventually, he has succumb to laughter, is noticed by Pearl and Hester, who join him on the scaffolding
  • The three hold hands which seems to alleviate some of his pain, and Pearl asks that they do this the next day at noon
  • He responds with some other time, the judgment day perhaps
  • Suddenly a meteor lights up the sky and an A appears in the sky
  • Then with the new light in the sky Chillingworth is seen in the distance, watching the family
  • Dimmesdale wonders aloud who he really is, but Hester can not say and Pearl will not tell him because he will not stand on the scaffolding with them during the day
  • Chillingworth approaches and escorts Dimmesdale home pretending he is none the wiser of what was occurring and suggesting perhaps Dimmesdale was sleep walking
  • The next day at church Dimmesdale gives his most powerful sermon ever, but afterwards the sexton hands Dimmesdale a black glove belonging to him, as it was found at the scaffolding, yet nothing is believed out of the ordinary
  • The A in the sky is also brought up, but the community believes it stands for Angel, as one of the old Governors died the previous night, that’s why Chillingworth and Hester were out so late
  • Chapter #13
  • 7 years has passed since the adultery has occurred and Hester has grown in the community significantly
  • She is a much more active and accepted member of society however she is still scorned
  • Hester has also changed as a person, not quite the open minded and resilient women she was before
  • She worries greatly about Dimmesdale and the harm she is causing by not telling him of Chillingworth’s identity
  • She also wonders what it means in her society to be a women
  • Chapter #14
  • Hester and Chillingworth have a conversation, she asks that he stops tormenting Dimmesdale, even saying that they should tell him of Chillingworth’s real identity
  • Chillingworth fakes friendliness during there conversation by saying that he has heard that perhaps Hester’s scarlet letter will be removed, but she says only divine power can do that
  • At the conclusion of there conversation it is clear to Chillingworth that Hester and Dimmesdale were lovers
  • Chillingworth is also aware of the evil that he has become for the first time, yet he is unwilling to try and become human again, he tells Hester that they are fated, her to be silent, and he to be a vengeful fiend, and that they should go there separate ways and live there life like it was destined
  • Chapter #15
  • Hester heads to find Pearl, she also thinks to herself that she hates her husband, no matter how wrong that may be, and that love was only a delusion
  • When she finds Pearl she notices Pearl has put an A on her chest with eelgrass
  • Hester questions Pearl, and they discuss the letter
  • Pearl makes a connection with the letter and Dimmesdale often clutching at his chest
  • Hester is unnerved by Pearl’s connections being made, and decides to avoid the topic as she is too young, even though Pearl pushes her to discuss it for the next few days
  • Chapter #16
  • Hester plans to tell Dimmesdale of Chillingworth’s identity so she goes to the forest with Pearl to wait for him as he will be passing through
  • Pearl questions Hester while waiting about the “black man” which she thinks connects to the scarlet letter
  • Pearl heard about this from Mistress Hibbins, and wants to know if it is true, Hester however denies it
  • Eventually Dimmesdale approaches, and Pearl wonders if it is the “black man’’ yet Hester again says it is not
  • Pearl questions though if he clutches his heart because the “black man” has made a mark over it much like Hester’s
  • Chapter #17
  • Hester and Dimmesdale finally talk, and he learns of Chillingworth’s identity
  • Although first, extremely mad, and blaming Hester for his suffering, he shifts his view after Hester clutches him and asks for forgiveness
  • They both decide that Chillingworth is by far a bigger sinner than either of them, they do worry however he will reveal there secret
  • They plan to eventually leave together for Europe where they can live as a family
  • Chapter #18
  • They are both thrilled and energized by there plan, Hester removes the scarlet letter and lets down her hair
  • There is an immediate feeling of a weight being lifted off of her, and also the forest is immediately brightened
  • They call over Pearl where she will finally meet her father, as her father, and family can become one
  • Chapter #19
  • Pearl refuses to come over to them when she is called, she is wary, as her mother has removed the letter
  • After making herself appear as her old, beaten self, Pearl finally comes over, and she kisses Hester and the letter
  • Hester tries to get Pearl to embrace Dimmesdale, but does not yet tell her he is her father
  • Pearl asks they all walk back together hand in hand, but Dimmesdale does not agree, upsetting Pearl, who runs to the creek to wash off her forehead where Dimmesdale kissed her
  • Chapter #20
  • When Dimmesdale gets back into Boston he is amazed at his twist in fate
  • Hester has become friends with some men who are taking a ship to Europe in a few days, and Hester plans to leave with them and Dimmesdale
  • On his way back Dimmesdale is so overjoyed he almost blurts out blasphemy, he nearly gives himself away to townspeople seeking his help, however restrains himself
  • Dimmesdale finally returns home where he tells Chillingworth he is no longer needed, he then turns to his sermon he had been writing for election day, burns it, and starts over and writes an even better one
  • Chapter #21
  • There is another public gathering, this time to inaugurate the new Governor
  • It is quite the festivity as everyone is celebrating and enjoying themselves, sailors, natives, townsfolk all gather together
  • Hester and Pearl are lost in the crowd, Pearl wonders whether the minister will hold out his hand to them like he did in the brook
  • Hester is lost in thought, dreaming of her freedom, when she is informed that Chillingworth will be aboard the ship since they need a doctor
  • As she looks across the crowd she sees him smirking at her
  • Chapter #22
  • There are armored soldiers in the procession, followed by the towns followers
  • Hester sees Dimmesdale looking healthier and happier than ever, it seems Pearl barely even recognizes him, however she tells Hester she plans to give him a kiss, but Hester scolds her
  • Hester begins to wonder if they are doing the right thing, it seems even though Dimmesdale is happy, he is very remote
  • Mistress Hibbins approaches Hester saying that Dimmesdale has the mark of the “black man” and soon everyone will know, she then suggests the Devil is Pearl’s father
  • We learn that Hibbins will soon be executed for being a witch
  • Hibbins leaves and Hester is standing at the foot of the scaffolding listening to Dimmesdale’s sermon when she is interrupted by Pearl
  • Pearl says that Chillingworth will be taking care of Dimmesdale’s passage on the boat and that she is to only worry about her and Pearl
  • When she finally looks back up at the sermon she realizes everyone is starring at her
  • Chapter #23
  • Dimmesdale’s sermon ends, leaving everyone amazed as they feel it was his best one yet
  • He tells of how New England’s people are the chosen ones, leaving everyone feeling uplifted
  • Everyone files out of the meeting hall to go to the evening’s feast, Dimmesdale takes this time to call Hester and Pearl to the scaffold ignoring Chillingworth’s attempts to stop him
  • Everyone looks up at him as this is occurring, and they realize he is confessing that he was Hester’s lover
  • He then says he is marked like Hester and rips open his shirt shocking everyone, he falls to his knees, and Pearl finally returns his kiss
  • Then, in Hester’s arms, she asks if they will spend the afterlife together, he is unsure as to whether God will judge there sins harshly enough to keep them apart
  • He then falls over and dies, leaving everyone in shock
  • Chapter #24
  • Following Dimmesdale’s death there is much discussion as to what people saw on his chest, some saw a scarlet letter like Hester’s, yet it is uncertain whether it was Chillingworth’s doing, Dimmesdale’s punishment to himself, or divine acts
  • Others believe they saw nothing on his chest and his only revelation was that he was guilty just as Hester, however the narrator believes those people are just trying to protect his reputation
  • Chillingworth soon dies because he is left with no reason to live anymore, he leaves an inheritance to Pearl
  • Hester and Pearl eventually disappear, and the town preserves the scaffold and her home as a testament to the legend of the scarlet letter
  • Many years later Hester returns alone, participating in charity work in the town, still wearing the scarlet letter, however it has lost all significance
  • When she dies she is buried in the King’s Chapel Graveyard, she is buried close to Dimmesdale
  • The two share a tombstone, marked simply with a scarlet letter “A”
